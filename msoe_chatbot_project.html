MSOE AI Chatbot

For a Conversation Design class, I collaborated on a project to develop an AI chatbot prototype tailored for Milwaukee School of Engineering (MSOE). We were tasked with designing a chatbot with a specific use case to help students. Our aim was to help students access MSOE’s health and support services. Over the course of a semester, I was the team lead responsible for:

  • Coordinating meetings and meeting plans
  • Managing project objectives and deadlines
  • Determining research objectives
  • Help design and prototype the AI chatbot

Our early user research revealed to us that it is not uncommon for students to either be uncomfortable with or unsure how to reach out to the university’s physical and mental health support services. An AI chatbot seemed like the perfect canvas for providing a tool where students can ask questions in a way that is as stress and anxiety free as possible.

From here we determined exactly what support services MSOE offered. Our chatbot focused on connecting students to the University’s Wellness Center and Raider Center for Academic Succes (RCAS). The Wellness Center specializes in helping students improve their physical and mental health while RCAS helps student with their academic health. We did not want to be liable for providing any suggestions to improve a student’s health as that type of advice should only come from one of MSOE’s many support professionals.

The personality of our chatbot had to be friendly, empathetic, and informational. We wanted a name and avatar for the chatbot that reflected these traits and hopefully comforted students as they asked it questions. The chatbot was named WISE, an acronym for Wellness, Information, Support, and Education, and was given an avatar of a cute and welcoming owl.

The conversation design of WISE was a long process with several iterations of conversation flows. Starting in Figma we outlined WISE’s prompts and how users would interact with it. Eventually we moved this conversation flow into Voiceflow, an AI chatbot prototyping tool. Each iteration was refined with information gathered by feedback from user testing and interviews with a representative from MSOE’s marketing department.

In a comprehensive presentation showcasing WISE’s capabilities and potential impact on improving the health of students, the final prototype was demonstrated to the class and MSOE’s marketing department. Receiving positive feedback, the chatbot effectively highlighted how connecting students to the health and support services at MSOE can improve their overall experience and engagement.
