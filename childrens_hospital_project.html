As part of a design project, we were tasked with redesigning the trauma room at Children's Hospital with a specific focus on optimizing the layout and organization to enhance the efficiency of thoracotomy procedures. This project required gaining insights into the challenges and requirements from hospital staff and collaborating with stakeholders to devise innovative solutions. The project duration spanned several months, and I worked closely with a multidisciplinary team to address the complex needs of the trauma room.

Our primary tasks involved conducting user interviews with hospital staff to understand their challenges and requirements, devising short-term and long-term solutions for optimizing the trauma room layout, and ensuring seamless workflow and accessibility to essential equipment during thoracotomy procedures. To accomplish this, we utilized skills in user research, space planning, medical equipment knowledge, and stakeholder collaboration. The goal was to prioritize patient care and medical efficiency while improving the overall functionality of the trauma room.

List of tasks I was responsible for completing:
  • Conducted user interviews with hospital staff, including physicians, nurses, and support staff, to gather insights into the challenges and requirements of thoracotomy procedures in the trauma room.
  • Analyzed the findings from the user interviews to identify pain points and opportunities for improvement in the current layout and organization of the trauma room.
  • Collaborated with a multidisciplinary team to devise both short-term and long-term solutions for optimizing the trauma room layout, including innovative floor plans tailored to the specific needs of thoracotomy procedures.
  • Utilized space planning principles to ensure a seamless workflow and accessibility to essential equipment within the redesigned trauma room, prioritizing patient care and medical efficiency.
  • Created visualizations of the proposed redesign to communicate the concepts effectively to stakeholders and solicit feedback.
  • Presented the proposed redesign to representatives from Children's Hospital, facilitating a healthy collaborative dialogue towards implementing impactful changes based on their input and feedback.

The Trauma Room Redesign project resulted in several positive outcomes:

  • The proposed redesign addressed the challenges and requirements identified through user interviews, providing innovative solutions tailored to the specific needs of thoracotomy procedures.
  • The collaboration with stakeholders from Children's Hospital facilitated a healthy dialogue and consensus towards implementing impactful changes in the trauma room.
  • The emphasis on prioritizing patient care and medical efficiency led to a streamlined workflow and improved accessibility to essential equipment, ultimately enhancing the efficiency of thoracotomy procedures.
  • Through this project, I gained valuable experience in user research, space planning, stakeholder collaboration, and presentation skills.

Overall, the Trauma Room Redesign project demonstrated the importance of user-centered design and collaboration in addressing complex challenges in healthcare environments, while also providing valuable learning experiences and skill development opportunities for the project team.
