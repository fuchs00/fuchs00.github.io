Children's Hospital Trauma Room Redesign

  • Overview

    As part of a class design project, we were tasked with redesigning the trauma room at Children's Hospital with a specific focus on optimizing the layout and organization to enhance the efficiency of thoracotomy procedures. We were tasked with creating short-term and long-term solutions for optimizing the trauma room layout that ensures a seamless workflow with accessibility to essential equipment. The project spanned several months, and I worked closely with a multidisciplinary team responsible for:

    • Determining research objectives
    • Identifying challenges and requirements from hospital staff
    • Planning an optimal and efficient room layout

    Gathering Information

    With Children’s Hospital undergoing renovations, the hospital staff recognized that this would be a good opportunity to address some of the inefficiencies they have in their trauma wing. Specifically, they identified the thoracotomy procedure as an area that needs to improve. Our team started the project by doing research about the procedure itself as well as the roles and responsibilities of the different staff members that conduct the procedure.

    The next step was to go through several stages of user interviews, talking to staff member of various roles to identify pain points and opportunities for improvement in the current layout and organization of the trauma room. Some of the problems we found were that the equipment for the procedure were not always easily identifiable, the storage for the equipment could be more easily accessible, and there is not always enough space to move around the trauma room due to the amount of people working and observing.

    Creating Solutions

    Between each round of interviews, we brainstormed short-term and long-term solutions for optimizing the trauma room layout, including innovative floor plans tailored to the specific needs of thoracotomy procedures. The short-term improvements were constrained to the size and location of the trauma room that was currently in use while the long-term improvements had much more freedom for the entire design of the room.

    We created visualizations of our proposed redesigns to effectively communicate our concepts to stakeholders from Children’s Hospital. Our short-term solution revised the organization of essential equipment to maximize the space of the current trauma room. The proposed room for our long-term solution includes similar organization for essential equipment and is almost twice the size of the current trauma room, allowing for a more comfortable amount of space for those performing the procedure as well as observing.

    Outcomes

    Our proposed redesigns were presented to a team of representatives from Children’s Hospital, highlighting how these changes can improve the pain points from thoracotomy procedures that they experience. This direct collaboration with stakeholders helped bring forward a deeper understanding of how our ideas can impact people in an area as influential as a trauma room.
